# Question 5: Identify different roles in Data Science(e.g., Data Analyst, Data Scientist, ML Engineer) and their responsibilties.


Data Science is a team sport. While the titles are often used interchangeably, there are distinct differences in focus and skill sets between the three primary roles.

    -  **Data Analyst** 
    *Focus: "What happened?" (Descriptive Analytics)* 
    A Data Analyst is the detective of the team. They focus on processing historical data to identify trends, patterns, and insights that can answer specific business questions immediately. They bridge the gap between raw data and business stakeholders.
        -  **Responsibilities:**
        -  Cleaning and organizing raw data (Data Wrangling).
        -  Writing complex SQL queries to extract data from databases.
        -  Creating visual dashboards and reports using tools like Tableau, PowerBI, or Excel.
        -  Communicating findings to non-technical teams to guide business strategy.

    -  **Data Scientist** 
    *Focus: "What will happen?" (Predictive Analytics)* 
    A Data Scientist is the innovator. They use advanced mathematics, statistics, and machine learning to build models that can predict future outcomes. Their work is more experimental and research-heavy than that of an analyst.
        -  **Responsibilities:**
        -  Formulating and testing hypotheses (A/B testing).
        -  Building and training predictive models (e.g., predicting customer churn).
        -  Selecting the appropriate machine learning algorithms for specific problems.
        -  Optimizing model accuracy using hyperparameter tuning.

    -  **Machine Learning (ML) Engineer** 
    *Focus: "How do we make it work at scale?" (Production Engineering)* 
    An ML Engineer is the builder. Once a Data Scientist proves that a model works in a lab setting, the ML Engineer takes that model and turns it into a scalable software product that can handle millions of users. They sit at the intersection of Data Science and DevOps.
        -  **Responsibilities:**
        -  Deploying machine learning models into production environments.
        -  Building and maintaining scalable data pipelines (ETL).
        -  Monitoring model performance and "drift" over time.
        -  Optimizing code for latency and speed (ensuring the model runs fast in real-time).
