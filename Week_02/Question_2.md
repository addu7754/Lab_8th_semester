# Question 2: Identify and describe any five real world datasets.


Real-world datasets are essential for training and testing machine learning models. Below are five of the most widely used datasets in the data science community, covering various domains such as biology, history, computer vision, and transportation.

    -  **The Iris Flower Dataset** 
    *Domain: Biology / Pattern Recognition* 
    Considered the "Hello World" of data science, this dataset was introduced by the statistician Ronald Fisher in 1936. It consists of 150 samples from three distinct species of Iris flowers (Setosa, Versicolor, and Virginica).
        -  **Features:** It contains four features: Sepal Length, Sepal Width, Petal Length, and Petal Width.
        -  **Usage:** It is primarily used for learning supervised machine learning algorithms, specifically multi-class classification problems.

    -  **The Titanic Dataset** 
    *Domain: History / Risk Analysis* 
    Sourced from the tragic sinking of the RMS Titanic in 1912, this dataset allows students to predict which passengers were likely to survive based on their personal characteristics.
        -  **Features:** Includes variables such as Passenger Class, Name, Sex, Age, Siblings/Spouses aboard, Parents/Children aboard, and Fare price.
        -  **Usage:** Ideally used for binary classification (Survived vs. Deceased) and practicing data cleaning techniques (handling missing values).

    -  **MNIST Database of Handwritten Digits** 
    *Domain: Computer Vision* 
    The MNIST (Modified National Institute of Standards and Technology) dataset is the standard benchmark for image processing systems. It contains 70,000 grayscale images of handwritten digits (0 through 9).
        -  **Features:** Each image is a $28 \times 28$ pixel matrix representing a single digit.
        -  **Usage:** It is the go-to dataset for training Neural Networks and Deep Learning models to recognize optical characters.

    -  **COVID-19 Global Case Data (JHU/WHO)** 
    *Domain: Healthcare / Epidemiology* 
    Since 2020, Johns Hopkins University and the WHO have maintained comprehensive datasets tracking the pandemic. These are time-series datasets that change daily.
        -  **Features:** Confirmed cases, deaths, and recoveries, organized by country, region, and date.
        -  **Usage:** Used for time-series forecasting, data visualization (dashboards), and predictive modeling of disease spread.

    -  **Uber Pickups in New York City** 
    *Domain: Transportation / Geospatial Analysis* 
    This dataset contains data on over 4.5 million Uber pickups in New York City. It provides granular data on urban movement and traffic patterns.
        -  **Features:** Date/Time of pickup, Latitude, Longitude, and the base company code.
        -  **Usage:** Essential for spatial data analysis, clustering (finding "hotspots" for rides), and heat-map visualization.
